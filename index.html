About Bug

Hi, I'm Castiel or Bug in a Tub. I'm an EWU student majoring in Visual Communication Design with a certificate in Animation. My passions lie in art, animation, and books. A bit of a mixed bag with the arts, I enjoy drawing, writing, and 3D modeling.
My goals currently are graduating and find a job in either Graphic Design or 3D modeling while working on my personal web comic project. My longterm goal is to one day become a published author.

What do you know now that you didn't know in January

Write a few brief phrases or sentences regarding what you learned, why it is/was important to you and what the outcome was.

I learned how to use flexbox and how useful it is to allow seamless refitting based on what is being used to view the site. I'm still very used to old school position stuff since I'm mainly a pc user, so I've realized that you must code for various different sized viewing.


Change and Growth

Describe 3 experiences when you learned something that changed how you thought about: thinking, design, education, and learning

1. Coding an actual webpage versus a petpage on a petite is vastly different so having to account for new things such as an overall nav bar and no extra information to format around (pet stats, pet minion etc).

2. Media queries. I'm heavily a pc user only (3 monitors) so I don't ever think or care about mobile/tablet versions (I rarely use my phone except for when I'm at work or class and the tablet I have is just for drawing on the go) so mobile/tablet design is never in my mind at all.

3. Site design is very different from how it used to be years ago and most sites are basic and straight to the point with the landing page being a tagline text and clean white background. Texture seems to not be something that's seen anymore or heavy use of decorative things just as frames and little bullets.
